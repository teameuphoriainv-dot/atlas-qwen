Meet Atlas: an autopilot agent for the electronic health record. One model acts. Another model objects. And the clinician always holds the pen.

Here's the problem. A doctor can say what they want in one sentence. But the software makes them click fourteen times to do it. Order entry, dozens of times a day. Clinicians now spend more of the day driving the record than talking to patients. So we built an agent that does the driving.

Watch it work. The clinician just types: order a CBC and a chest x-ray, and start metformin five hundred milligrams twice daily. A smart router picks the right Qwen model. The agent reads the chart over FHIR, reasons over it, and drafts three correctly coded orders. It even cites the evidence: she's on lisinopril, she has type two diabetes, so metformin is appropriate. One click confirms, and the orders write back to the chart. And the model never saw a name, an MRN, or a date of birth. That boundary is enforced by a test that fails the build.

But drafting is the easy part. Trust is the hard part. So before the clinician ever sees a proposal, a second, independent Qwen model reviews it. Layer one is deterministic code: allergy conflicts and duplicates, caught without a model. Layer two is qwen-max, prompted to refute. Here, the agent proposed amoxicillin for a patient with a documented penicillin allergy. The Sentinel catches the cross-reactivity, blocks it, and forces a deliberate override. The author never grades its own homework.

Under the hood, Atlas orchestrates four Qwen models on Alibaba Cloud Model Studio. A router sends each request to turbo, plus, or max by complexity. qwen-max drafts orders and runs the Sentinel. And qwen-vl-max turns a photo of a paper medication list into structured, reconciled proposals. Reads auto-execute. Writes only ever queue. And the whole toolset is exposed over MCP, so any agent can reuse it.

And then there's the unglamorous sixty percent that makes it real. PHI isolation, enforced in code. Structural validation at the write boundary. A tamper-evident, hash-chained audit log. Resilience on every model call. Evidence provenance on every claim. Thirty tests, green on every push.

Because autonomy in medicine isn't one brilliant model. It's a system: a model that acts, a model that objects, deterministic rails around both, and a human holding the pen. Atlas. Built on Qwen, on Alibaba Cloud.
